TASK TITLE   : Top Cuisines
OBJECTIVE    : Identify the top three most common cuisines in the restaurant
               dataset and calculate what percentage of restaurants serve each.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 1 — UNDERSTANDING THE TASK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The restaurant dataset contains records of thousands of dining establishments
from across the world. Each restaurant entry includes a "Cuisines" field that
lists one or more types of cuisine the restaurant serves — for example:
"North Indian, Chinese" or "Fast Food, Italian".

The task has two clear parts:

  PART A → Find the TOP THREE most frequently occurring cuisines.
  PART B → Calculate what PERCENTAGE of restaurants serve each of those
            top three cuisines.

This is a classic frequency analysis problem, commonly performed in business
intelligence and data analytics to understand market composition.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 2 — DATA PREPARATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Before performing any analysis, the dataset must be prepared:

  Step 1 — Load the CSV file into a structured DataFrame using pandas.

  Step 2 — Identify and handle missing values. The Cuisines column contained
            9 null entries (out of 9,551 total records). These 9 rows were
            excluded from the analysis, leaving 9,542 valid records.

  Step 3 — Handle multi-cuisine entries. A restaurant may serve more than one
            cuisine type. The Cuisines field stores these as a comma-separated
            string (e.g., "Japanese, Sushi, Asian"). To count individual
            cuisines accurately, each entry was split on the comma delimiter
            and then "exploded" — meaning each cuisine became its own
            individual row for counting purposes.

  Step 4 — Strip any extra whitespace from cuisine names to ensure consistent
            matching (e.g., " Chinese" and "Chinese" are treated as the same).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 3 — ANALYSIS LOGIC
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Once the data was cleaned and prepared, the frequency of each unique cuisine
was counted across all restaurant entries (including multiple cuisines per
restaurant). The cuisines were then ranked in descending order of frequency,
and the top three were extracted.

Percentage Calculation Formula:

  Percentage = (Number of restaurants serving the cuisine / Total valid
                restaurant records) × 100

  NOTE: Since one restaurant can serve multiple cuisines, the same restaurant
  may be counted towards more than one cuisine. As a result, the three
  percentages will NOT add up to exactly 100%.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 5 — INTERPRETATION OF RESULTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  ► NORTH INDIAN (41.50%)
    North Indian cuisine is the most dominant in the dataset by a significant
    margin. Appearing in more than 4 out of every 10 restaurants, this cuisine
    type reflects the strong cultural presence and widespread consumer demand
    for North Indian food, particularly across South Asian markets.

  ► CHINESE (28.66%)
    Chinese cuisine holds a firm second position, present in roughly 3 out of
    every 10 restaurants. Its high prevalence underscores its international
    appeal and widespread adaptation into local dining cultures globally.

  ► FAST FOOD (20.81%)
    Fast Food rounds out the top three, appearing in approximately 1 in 5
    restaurants. This finding is consistent with global trends showing continued
    growth in quick-service and casual dining segments across urban centres.
